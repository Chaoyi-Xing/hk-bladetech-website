How can I extend the life of my blades?

We recommend two main strategies:

  • 1. Gap Setting: Ensure the rotor-stator gap is correct (0.2-0.3mm) to avoid "rubbing" which dulls edges quickly.
  • 2. Material Upgrade: If cutting abrasive plastic (like glass-filled nylon), standard D2 steel isn't enough. Ask us about Carbide-inlaid blades for 3-5x longer life.
How do I know when to replace my blades?

Watch for these signs:

  • Increased Dust/Fines: Dull blades shatter plastic rather than cutting it cleanly.
  • Higher Amp Draw: Your motor has to work harder to cut.
  • Increased Noise: Dull cutting creates more vibration and sound.

We recommend keeping a spare set on the shelf to minimize downtime.
