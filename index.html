How can I extend the life of my machine knives?

We recommend two primary strategies to maximize longevity:

  1. Precision Gap Setting: Calibrate the rotor-stator gap strictly to 0.2-0.3mm to avoid rubbing and rapid dulling.
  2. Material Upgrades: Standard D2 steel fails quickly on abrasive plastics (like glass-filled nylon). Upgrade to Carbide-inlaid blades for 3-5x longer life.
How do I know when to replace my blades?

Timely replacement protects your equipment. Watch for these warning signs:

  • Increased Burrs & Dust: Dull blades shatter plastic instead of cutting it cleanly.
  • Abnormal Noise & Vibration: Indicates micro-chipping. Continuing risks severe spindle damage.
  • Higher Amp Draw: Your motor is working significantly harder.
  • Visible Discoloration: Blue-purple oxidation indicates loss of physical properties due to high-friction heat.

Pro Tip: We highly recommend keeping a spare set of blades on the shelf to minimize costly machine downtime during changeovers.

Which blade material is right for my application?

Choosing the correct alloy lowers your cost-per-ton. Here is our quick guide:

  • Standard Plastics (PE/PP/PET): Use Premium D2 / SKD11 for excellent cost-performance.
  • Glass Fiber (10-30%): Standard D2 dulls in days. Upgrade to Powder Metallurgy or D2 with High-Wear Coating.
  • Highly Abrasive (>30% GF, Carbon Fiber): Use Tungsten Carbide-Inlaid Blades. Drops cost-per-ton by >50%.
  • Film & Foil Slitting: Use High-Speed Steel (HSS) + DLC Coating to prevent static and material adhesion.
